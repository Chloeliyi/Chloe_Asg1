History Of NCT

NCT

NCT (Korean: 엔시티; an acronym for Neo Culture Technology) is a South Korean boy band formed by SM Entertainment and introduced in January 2016. The group consists of 23 members as of April 2021, ranging in age from late teens to mid-twenties and divided into four different sub-units: NCT U, NCT 127, NCT Dream, and WayV

2016: Debuts of NCT U, NCT 127 and NCT Dream

On April 4, SM Entertainment announced the debut of NCT's first sub-unit, NCT U, which then consisted of six members: Taeil, Taeyong, Doyoung, Ten, Jaehyun and Mark.

On July 1, SM announced NCT's second sub-unit, NCT 127. The unit would be based in Seoul, the number "127" representing the longitude coordinate of the city. The unit debuted with seven members: Taeil, Taeyong, Yuta, Jaehyun, Winwin, Mark, and Haechan.

On August 18, SM announced that NCT's third sub-unit would be NCT Dream. The unit debuted with seven members: Mark, Renjun, Jeno, Haechan, Jaemin, Chenle and Jisung.
